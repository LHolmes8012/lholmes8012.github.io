Oaked vs. Unoaked

The Ultimate Chardonnay Showdown

What's the deal with unoaked?

Old World (particularly French) traditionalists do not age chardonnay in oak barrels, or do so for only a short period of time. This leads to a brighter, crisp wine without the depth of its oaked counterpart.

What's the deal with oaked?

New World chardonnays, particularly those from California, are commonly aged in oak barrels, giving them an "oaky" flavor. Other tasting notes related to oaked chardonnay include: toast, vanilla, nutty, creamy, etc.
